# Oishii

## What it is
[[oishii]] is a premium indoor-farming brand known for Japanese strawberry varietals sold in the U.S. market. In this source, it appears as both a produce brand and a packaging innovation case.

## Key facts from current source
- Selling premium pesticide-free, non-GMO Japanese varietal strawberries since 2018.
- Positions itself as the world's largest indoor vertical strawberry farm.
- Introduced the Nikko Berry as a more affordable, more widely distributed SKU.
- Launched a first-ever freshness guarantee alongside the new pack format.

## Packaging significance
- The Nikko Berry pack moves away from a conventional clear clamshell to a paperboard tray with film seal.
- The pack becomes a visible expression of Oishii's product-quality promise, not just a container.
- The case connects packaging sustainability, shelf-life control, and brand trust.

## Strategic reading
Oishii is relevant to the wiki because it sits at the intersection of premium produce branding and structural packaging change. The source suggests that packaging is part of the brand story: innovation is communicated through both materials and performance claims.
