Rollr’s packaging, designed by [Mother Design](https://www.motherlondon.com/), leans hard into fun, with a layout that feels structured yet full of energy. The rounded, sans serif type is clean and legible, paired with punchy iconography and confident use of negative space.

Colors skew pastel but not washed out, and there’s a satisfying contrast between soft tones and bold lines. Every label element looks and feels intentional, but never overly clinical.

*Rollr is a revolutionary new deodorant that marries an innovative format with luxury design cues and a unique sensorial user experience. Unlike other refillable deodorants within the category which focus almost solely on the functional product attributes and efficacy, Rollr elevates deodorant application to a pleasurable act of self care.*

*In the context of a personal care market with increasing consumer awareness and demand for sustainable products, and a growing and active competitor set, Rollr needed to redefine the category and disrupt the conventional category tropes.*

![](https://thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/2.-Rollr_Art-Direction.jpg.avif)

*Background & Market Context – Finding Opportunity for Growth  
While the deodorant category at large has generally been stuck in the past – cheap, plastic and an afterthought – there has been a significant shift in consumer attitudes and behaviour towards personal care products as a whole.  
Indeed the global sales of refillable deodorants is estimated to surge at a rate of 5.6% CAGR by 2033 as consumers become increasingly concerned about the environmental impact of their purchasing decisions. This is reflected in growing demand for recycled materials and a shift toward brands that prioritise sustainability.*

*In addition, consumers are actively seeking to avoid synthetic chemicals, parabens, and other potentially harmful substances. With concerns over the potential risks associated with deodorants containing aluminium rumbling on, the opportunity for naturally derived products is unquestionable.*

![](https://thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/5.-Rollr_Chatbot-tone-of-voice-and-gem-options.jpg.avif)

*Cue Milo Pinckney, who had an idea for an innovative, natural, sustainable, and refillable roll-on deodorant that leaves users feeling physically clean, fresh, and as if they aren’t wearing anything at all.*

*However, the deodorant category has already seen increasing disruption from brands challenging traditional formats and materials, including Fussy and Wild (UK), that have put sustainability at the forefront of deodorant design.*

*But while other brands have challenged the plastic-heavy deodorant category with refills, Milo wanted to go one step further – introducing a premium fragrance-grade roll-on that reinvents personal care as self-expression. The combination of a patented refillable design, natural ingredients, and luxury fragrance-inspired positioning sets Rollr apart from any of the existing products on the market that focus primarily on functional sustainability.*

![](https://thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/3.-Rollr_Art-Direction.png.avif)

*Product Effectiveness & Experience – The Last Deodorant You’ll Ever Buy*

*But compromising on efficacy was not an option. The product had to deliver on what consumers expect as a base line – protection from nasty odours and perspiration. Unlike deodorants that simply mask odour or antiperspirants that block sweat, Rollr introduces the first-ever combination of anti-odorant and de-perspirant ingredients – a water-activated, cooling crystal formula that refreshes skin while allowing it to breathe.*

*In addition, by allowing customers to add their own water at home, the patented refillable bottle drastically cuts the product’s environmental impact. This approach reduces the packaging required to make, ship and recycle the refills by 90%, compared to other products in the market. (Based on refill/formula weight (g/ml) of Rollr versus brands including Wild, Fussy, Dove, Mitchum, Sure, Nivea, Sanex and others).*

![](https://thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/6.-Rollr_Store-front.png.avif)

*Finally, the two most widely accepted product delivery mechanics are spray and roll on. Many of the new sustainably oriented brands already on the market come in stick or more divisively balm form, which adds to the consumer inertia when it comes to switching. Rollr is the first deodorant to use a steel or gemstone roller ball for application, further enhancing its premium credentials and the sensory experience.*

*But in a category which is hugely competitive, commoditised and dominated by big conventional brands such as Sure, Dove and Nivea, the challenge ahead is complex.*

*We needed to create a brand world that would clearly differentiate Rollr from existing products and brands in the category; create a sense of intrigue by challenging people’s perception of deodorant; and position it as an indulgent, luxury experience worth the price point.*

![](https://thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/8.-Rollr_Style-Guide-examples.jpg.avif)

*Objectives:*

- *To differentiate from category conventions: To create a memorable and distinctive brand world (bottle design, name, visual identity, tone of voice etc), setting itself apart from both mainstream and other sustainable / natural deodorant brands.*
- *To bring to life the sensory experience: The brand world needed to reflect the pleasurable and indulgent sensory experience delivered through the fine fragrance-inspired scents, gemstone roller balls, and luxurious bottle design.*
- *To appeal to the ”conscientious hedonist”: Rollr’s identified target consumer who values both pleasure and environmental responsibility, presenting Rollr as a product that satisfies both desires.*
- *To transform the perception of deodorant: Rollr is a departure from the image of deodorant as a cheap, plastic, functional product and instead is positioned as a luxury, fragrance-inspired act of self-care..*
- *To drive sales and retail distribution: Ultimately, the aim is to achieve business success by driving sales, expanding retail distribution and capturing market share.*

*Project Description & SoW:*

*With many natural deodorants already on the market, the immediate task was to determine what would truly differentiate the brand. We partnered with brand founder Pinckney and a business incubator to develop the brand idea and strategic direction of Rollr.*

*Our initial category audits showed that the natural deodorant market has often relied on outdated sustainability cues, with products that felt primarily functional. No brands were leaning into pleasure, despite the growing demand for sensorial and mind-body stimulation in beauty and personal care. As sustainability increasingly becomes a hygiene factor, Rollr taps into the shift towards products that balance responsibility and indulgence, with a product that can be proudly displayed alongside treasured fragrances rather than banished to the bathroom cabinet.*

**As well as the visual identity, we co-created Rollr’s distinctive bottle design with founder, Pinckney, product designer Joshua Oates, **3D designer Oskar Graziosi and Neuroaesthetic expert Saskia Wheeler****, *to create something that moves beyond the functional and disposable aesthetic of typical deodorants. Inspired by fine fragrance design, the smooth, luxurious sculpted glass bottle is intended to be an object of desire, kept and refilled indefinitely. This material choice immediately elevates Rollr beyond conventional deodorants, and gives the product a substantial and luxurious feel. A modular and patented screw-top neck allows for easy refilling (without leaks), reinforcing Rollr’s commitment to sustainability. Unlike plastic roll-ons, Rollr features a cooling steel roller ball, with optional gemstone upgrades—Rose Quartz, Green Aventurine, and Blue Dumortierite—that are intended to enhance the sensory experience while offering a feeling of exclusivity and personal customisation. The darkened glass bottle is complemented by light pastel tones, reflecting the natural ingredients of each scent, including mandarin, vetiver, and clary sage.*

![](https://thedieline.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/7.-Rollr_Overview.jpg.avif)

*The name ‘Rollr’, inspired by the product’s roller ball feature, is the only familiar aspect of the product, ensuring instant recognition amidst an otherwise unexpected reinvention of the category. The word was chosen to immediately communicate function, helping users understand its purpose before discovering how every other aspect challenges expectations. Removing the ’e’ gives it a modern, distinctive edge—maintaining familiarity while signalling a modern, distinctive edge that is both memorable and easily searchable.*

*The brand’s tone of voice reflects the mindset of a ‘conscientious hedonist’ – a persona we developed to describe someone who embraces pleasure while being mindful of environmental impact. The brand voice is bold and expressive, featuring phrases such as “Damn you smell good” and “For pleasure and planet.”*

*The logo is a custom-drawn wordmark with elegant serifs and a fluid curve extending from the bowl to the leg of the ‘R’, echoing the cooling, fluid nature of the product. The counter of the ‘o’ is rotated to suggest a rolling motion. The clean and modern Kalice typeface is used throughout the brand, providing a refined yet understated contrast to the more evocative elements of the identity.*

*The graphic language includes four expressive and abstract fluid letterforms, each representing one of the three scents available at launch – Vetiver, Clary Sage, and Mandarin. These icons and decorative patterns add a layer of visual interest, sensory evocation and brand cohesion. The fluid letterforms themselves elicit the sensory experience of using Rollr. Again, their organic, flowing shapes suggest Rollr’s liquid form and the rolling motion of application. The artistic and abstract quality of the scent identifiers also reinforces Rollr’s positioning as a premium, fragrance-inspired product. It moves away from the functional aesthetic of typical deodorants and aligns more with the visual language of high-end perfumes.*

**Inspired by luxury perfume campaigns, we developed an art direction and photography style that presents the product confidently lit and floating in space, resembling a jewel about to land in the user’s hand, **shot by Veega Studio***. Subtle touches of blur and shadow enhance the tactile and sensorial experience. Dreamlike imagery of outdoor landscapes is reflective of the sensory escapism that comes with indulging in pleasurable scents.*

*The use of a deep, rich burgundy hue combined with light pastel tones reflects the natural ingredients in the product, while creating a sophisticated and elegant aesthetic. This contrasts sharply with the often-bright and neutral colors of standard deodorants on the market. The brand’s motion identity uses a rippling, liquid animation style that enhances the sense of fluidity, pleasure, and sensory delight.*

*Summary & Achievements to date:*

*Rollr reimagines personal care as a sensorial ritual and sustainable practice. In doing so, Rollr is not only redefining the deodorant category but is sparking a broader conversation about sustainable luxury and conscious consumption. It has already resonated with consumers who are seeking out products that align with their values and aspirations, demonstrating that sustainability can be synonymous with sophistication. Rollr is a testament to the power of design to inspire positive change.*

*Since launching exclusively in the iconic John Bell & Croydon store in London in December 2024, the brand is going from strength to strength. Despite only a nominal investment of £2K on ad spend on social media, sales have surpassed expectations. At the time of writing, the brand was achieving sales of +100% month on month and its positive performance has in turn led to it expanding its retail distribution, securing listings in 4 additional luxury destinations including Estelle Manor and Gleneagles.*

*With one patent on the innovative lid design granted and a second pending, 2025 looks set to be an exciting year for Rollr and founder Milo.*
