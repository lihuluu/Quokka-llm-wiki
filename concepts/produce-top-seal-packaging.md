# Produce Top-Seal Packaging

## Definition
[[produce-top-seal-packaging]] is a produce-pack format that replaces a rigid plastic clamshell with a tray plus sealed film lid. In this Oishii case, the format uses SBS paperboard, a PET-based liner film, and engineered micro-perforation to balance protection, visibility, and respiration control.

## Why it matters
This format matters because it shows a realistic transition path away from all-plastic produce packs. It does not eliminate mixed materials, but it can sharply reduce plastic volume while also improving freshness management.

## Design logic
- Replace rigid clamshell walls with a lower-mass tray structure.
- Use top-seal film to keep berries protected while tuning oxygen and moisture transfer.
- Treat freshness life as part of sustainability performance, not a separate issue.
- Add clear consumer instructions when the pack requires disassembly across waste streams.

## Trade-offs
- Better plastic reduction than conventional clamshells.
- Better story for premium produce brands such as [[oishii]].
- Still limited by end-of-life friction when liners and pads are not widely recyclable.
- Sustainability claims can overstate consumer reality if disassembly behavior is unlikely.
